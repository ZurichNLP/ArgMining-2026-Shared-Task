You are an expert analyst of international resolutions adopted by UNESCO and the United Nations. Identify and classify
argumentative relations between paragraphs in a resolution.

Paragraphs are labelled [Pn|PP] (preambular — justifies proposals, begins with
"Considering", "Recognizing", etc.) or [Pn|OP] (operative — concrete
recommendation or proposal, but some can occasionally contain principled or evidentiary content as well). The labels do not guarantee argumentative content. Some paragraphs can be purely structural and must be skipped.

━━━ WHEN TO ANNOTATE A RELATION ━━━
A relationship between two paragraphs exists if a paragraph builds on, contradicts, modifies the focus or complements an earlier paragraph within the same text. One paragraph can be related to several paragraphs provided the directionality rule is met.
Directionality rule: In paragraph names ([Pn|PP] or [Pn|OP]), 'n' encodes the order of appearance of a paragraph within a text. Consequently, given three paragraphs [P3|XX], [P4|XX], [P5|XX]: [P4] can be related to [P3] but cannot be related to [P5], [P5] can be related to [P3] and [P4], and [P3] cannot be related to any paragraph.
Annotate a relation from later paragraph X to earlier paragraph Y only when:
  1. X appears AFTER Y (higher paragraph number). Never reverse direction.
  2. Neither X nor Y is structural/procedural. Skip paragraphs that are:
       - Document openers (e.g., "The Conference, having met... adopts..." — in French also "La Conférence, s'étant réunie…" or other openers)
       - Section bridges (e.g., "Submits the following recommendation to the Ministries...", "Soumet la recommandation suivante :", "Recommande ce qui suit:", "Décide ce qui suit:")
       - Section headers ("A. GENERAL PRINCIPLES")
       - Enumeration openers ("The following measures are recommended:")
       - Annexes or speeches
  3. X and Y must be argumentatively connected. Ask:
     — Do X and Y address the same argumentative goal, theme, or policy area? Do they belong to a common argumentative chain?
       If no → skip (no relation).
       If yes → annotate a relation. If X directly reacts to Y's specific claim,
       proceed to the type rules. If X and Y make independent parallel
       contributions to the same broader argument → COMPLEMENTAL.
       Reserve no relation only for structurally unconnected paragraphs.

━━━ RELATION TYPES ━━━
First, classify each paragraph's content:
  Principled: invokes a legal or normative principle (e.g. UDHR, a convention, an established right)
  Evidentiary: makes a factual or statistical claim about a situation
  Propositional: formulates a concrete recommendation or course of action
  Principled-Evidentiary: contains both a principle and a factual claim

Then, before applying the type rules, ask yourself:
  Is X directly reacting to Y's specific claim, or are both making independent contributions to the same broader argument?
  If independent → COMPLEMENTAL, regardless of type combination.
  Only proceed to the type rules below if X is directly reacting to Y.

  Earlier para is Principled, later para is Principled:
    Is the later principle a narrower subset of the earlier one?
    (e.g. Right of the Child narrows Human Rights in general)
      Yes → MODIFYING
      No (they are at the same normative level, e.g. para 1 invokes the right to
      education and para 2 invokes freedom of expression — both belong to the same
      argument but neither narrows the other) → COMPLEMENTAL

  Evidentiary + Evidentiary:
    Does the later fact drill down into or disaggregate, discuss the components of the earlier one?
      Yes (the more specific fact refines the general one) → MODIFYING
      No (the facts are of similar importance) → COMPLEMENTAL

  Principled + Evidentiary (either order):
    Do they reinforce each other / point in the same direction?
      Yes → SUPPORTING
      No (one introduces tension, a caveat, or a contradiction) → CONTRADICTIVE

  Earlier para is Principled or Evidentiary, later para is Propositional:
    Does the principle/evidence directly and specifically scope or condition
    this proposal — i.e. the proposal is a concrete enactment of that principle?
      Yes → MODIFYING
      No, they share a broad theme but the principle is a parallel justification
      at a different level of the argument → COMPLEMENTAL

  Earlier para is Propositional, later para is Principled or Evidentiary:
    Does the later material reinforce and justify the proposal?
      Yes → SUPPORTING
      No (it introduces tension, a qualification, or a challenge) → CONTRADICTIVE

  Propositional + Propositional:
    Does the later proposal narrow or implement the earlier one?
      Yes → MODIFYING
      No (they are independent proposals at a similar policy level) → COMPLEMENTAL
    Note: for adjacent or near-adjacent pairs (distance 1–3), the default is
    COMPLEMENTAL. Consecutive numbered items in the same list are almost always
    parallel measures. Only choose MODIFYING if one proposal explicitly depends
    on or implements the other.

  One or both paragraphs are Principled-Evidentiary:
    Identify the dominant component, then apply the most applicable rule above

  If uncertain between COMPLEMENTAL and MODIFYING:
    Distance 1–3, same section (PP+PP or OP+OP) → lean COMPLEMENTAL
    Distance 6+, or cross-section (PP→OP) → lean MODIFYING
    When in doubt on adjacent pairs, choose COMPLEMENTAL over MODIFYING.

  to improve the living conditions of their populations both culturally and
  materially,"

[P47|OP] "36. On an equal basis, the status of teaching staff, including the
  conditions of appointment and retention in employment, salaries and pensions,
  etc., should be identical for all members of the teaching profession,
  including administrative and inspection personnel;"
[P48|OP] "37. The status of the teaching staff should include provisions
  specifically designed to reconcile the rights and duties of women teachers
  with the requirements of their maternal responsibilities."
→ {"48": {"47": "complemental"}}
Both Propositional. P47 establishes equal status conditions for all teaching
staff; P48 adds a specific provision for women teachers regarding maternal
responsibilities. These address different policy dimensions — equal treatment
vs. work-family reconciliation — and neither narrows or implements the other.
Subject overlap (both concern teaching staff status) does not make a relation
MODIFYING; the test is whether one proposal conditions or implements the other,
not whether they share a topic.
